Basic Introductions & Greetings

Very simple conversation starters for brand‑new learners.

In this lesson, we will practice basic introductions and greetings.
Each phrase has audio, click 🔊 to hear it.
Transliterations [an approximate spelling using English letters] are also given, but try reading in Russian first.

Formal vs. Informal

  • Ты is informal “you” (friends, kids, people you know well).
    Вы is formal or plural “you” (new people, elders, polite situations).
  • Здравствуйте [Zdrástvuyt'e] — a polite “Hello.”
    Привет [Pr'iv'et] — friendly “Hi.”
  • Asking a name:
    Как вас зовут? [Kak vas zovút?] (formal) vs.
    Как тебя зовут? [Kak t'eb'a zovut?] (informal).
  • Saying goodbye:
    До свидания [Do sv'idán'iya] (neutral/formal) vs.
    Пока [Poka] (casual “Bye”).

Tip: When in doubt, start formal (Вы / Здравствуйте). People will switch you to informal if appropriate.

Essential Phrases

Minimal suggested phrases for your first practice session are highlighted in orange.
  • Здра́вствуйте [Zdrástvuyt'e] — Hello (formal/polite)
  • Как вас зову́т? [Kak vas zovút?] — What is your name? (formal)
  • Меня́ зову́т Яков. [Men'á zovút Yakov.] — My name is Yakov.
  • Прия́тно познако́миться. [Pr'iyátno poznakóm'its'a.] — Nice to meet you.
  • Очень прия́тно. [Ochen' pr'iyátno.] — (It’s) very nice (to meet you).
  • До свида́ния. [Do sv'idán'iya.] — Goodbye.
  • До ско́рой встре́чи. [Do skóroy vstr'échi.] — See you soon.
  • Приве́т! [Pr'iv'e't!] — Hi!
  • Как дела́? [Kak d'ela'?] — How are you?
  • Спаси́бо, хорошо́. [Spas'íbo, khoroshó.] — Thanks, (I’m) good.
  • Как ва́ши дела́? [Kak váshi d'elá?] — How are you? (formal)
  • Пока́! [Poka'!] — Bye!
  • Спаси́бо. [Spas'i'bo.] — Thank you.
  • Пожа́луйста. [Pozháluysta.] — Please / You’re welcome.
  • С пра́здником! [S pra'zdn'ikom!] — Happy holiday!
