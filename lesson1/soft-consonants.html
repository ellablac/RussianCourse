Deep dive into grammar rule: All cases when consonants are soft

Always soft consonants:

  • й, ч, щ — always soft.

Consonants are soft:

  • if followed by the letters е, ё, и, ю, я (they trigger palatalization /'/ on the preceding consonant);
  • if followed by the soft sign ь.

When paired hard consonants soften before soft consonants:

  • /z, s, n/ become /z', s', n'/ before /d', t', z', s', n'/, e.g.: месть [m'es't'], здесь [z'd'es'], бантик [ˈban't'ɪk]. /n/ also softens before /t͡ɕ/, /ɕː/, e.g.: кончик [ˈkon'chɪk], гонщик [ˈgon'shchːɪk].
  • /z, s, n/ become /z', s', n'/ before /b', p', v', f'/, e.g.: зверь [z'v'er'], свет often [s'v'et] (though sometimes [sv'et]).
