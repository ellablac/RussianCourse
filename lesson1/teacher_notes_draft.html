Teacher Notes: Vowels Ё, И, Ы, Э, Ю, Я

Quick reference for adult-American learners. Use with your mnemonic cards and articulation cues.

И
Ы

Ё ё

  • Core sound: yo (/ʲo/ after consonants; /jo/ word-initial or after vowels/Ь).
  • Stress: Ё is always stressed. That helps learners hear it clearly.
  • Softening: After a consonant, Ё softens (palatalizes) the consonant: лё ~ lyo.
  • Mnemonic (example): A professor (or “yo!” face) pointing at dots above the letter, saying “yo.”
Common pitfalls: Don’t read Ё as Е. Don’t de-stress it. Keep a clear “yo,” not “yo-uh.”

Drills

  • Choral: ё–ё–ё; мё, лё, нё
  • Minimal pairs: все (fsye) vs всё (fsyo); ел vs ёл
  • Words: всё, ёлка, ёж, трёх

Teacher script (quick)

  1. “Ё is always stressed; think bright yo.”
  2. Model “мё—лё—нё” (class repeats). Point out softened consonant before Ё.
  3. Contrast with Е: “ме” vs “мё” → glide + rounding on Ё.

И и

  • Core sound: long, pure “ee” (/i/), as in machine.
  • Softening: Usually softens the preceding consonant: ми, ли, ни.
  • Mnemonic: Rollercoaster drop (slanted line) between two supports; kids shout “eee!”
  • Articulation: Lips lightly spread, tongue high and forward. No initial “y” glide.
Common pitfalls: Avoid English “ih.” Keep it a steady “ee,” no “yee.”

Drills

  • Choral: и–и–и; ми—ни—ли
  • Contrast with Ы: ми (mee) vs мы (“iuh”)
  • Words: мир, рис, игра, интернет

Teacher script (quick)

  1. “Straight to ee, no ‘y’.” Echo 2–3×.
  2. Air-trace the coaster drop; slide voice “eeee.”
  3. Contrast with Ы using “ми/мы.”

Ы ы

  • Core sound: Russian /ɨ/ (no exact English match). Teach as a firm, centered “iuh” in one steady sound.
  • Hard counterpart: Keeps the preceding consonant hard (contrast with И).
  • Mnemonic: Letter with a cane, a bit unwell; sighs “iuh.”
  • Articulation: Lips neutral (no big smile), tongue high but slightly retracted.
Common pitfalls: Don’t say pure “ee” or a relaxed “uh.” No “y” at the start.

Drills

  • Choral: ы–ы–ы; мы—ты—сын—рыба
  • Minimal pairs: ми vs мы; ли vs лы
  • Words: мы, сыры, сыт, крыша

Teacher script (quick)

  1. “Center the sound: iuh—lips neutral.”
  2. Mirror mouth shapes: И (smile) vs Ы (neutral/retracted).
  3. Rapid switches: ми—мы—ми—мы.

Э э

  • Core sound: short, clean eh (/e/) as in bet, met.
  • No softening: Э does not soften the preceding consonant; no “y” glide.
  • Mnemonic: Big ear shaped like Э; hand to ear saying “eh.”
  • Articulation: Lips relaxed, jaw slightly open; one vowel, no slide to “ay.”
Common pitfalls: Don’t say “yeh” (that’s Е). Don’t glide toward “ay.”

Drills

  • Choral: э–э–э; мэ—нэ—лэ
  • Contrast with Е: ме (with glide/softening) vs мэ (hard, clean)
  • Words: это, эхо, этаж, мэр, Мэри

Teacher script (quick)

  1. “Hear the ear: short, clean eh.”
  2. Board contrast: Е vs Э; choral “ме—мэ—ме—мэ.”
  3. Kinesthetic: cup ear; class says “eh.”

Ю ю

  • Core sound: yu (~ /ju/) word-initially/after vowels/Ь; after consonants: softens the consonant + gives /ʲu/ (“lyu”, “nyu”).
  • Rule of position: Start of word or after vowel/Ь → “y-u.” After consonant → soften + “u.”
  • Articulation: Lip rounding for “u,” tongue fronted when softening a consonant.
  • Mnemonic (optional): Comet/rocket “yu!” or a bubble “u” with a tiny “y” hook.
Common pitfalls: Don’t drop the softening after a consonant; don’t say plain English “you” with a full diphthong.

Drills

  • Choral: ю–ю–ю; лю—ню—тю
  • Position contrast: Юра (Yura) vs люкс (lyuks)
  • Words: люблю, Юля, юг, тюль

Teacher script (quick)

  1. “Word start: ‘yu.’ After consonant: soften + ‘u’ (lyu/nyu).”
  2. Echo “лю—ню—тю,” feel the soft consonant.
  3. Minimal pair building with board syllables.

Я я

  • Core sound: ya (~ /ja/) word-initially/after vowels/Ь; after consonants: softens + “a” (/ʲa/).
  • Rule of position: Start of word or after vowel/Ь → “y-a.” After consonant → soften + “a.”
  • Articulation: Open “a” vowel; tongue/front engagement when softening the consonant.
  • Mnemonic (optional): “Yawn”—big open mouth “ya.”
Common pitfalls: Don’t say plain English “ya” everywhere; remember the softening after consonants.

Drills

  • Choral: я–я–я; мя—ня—ля
  • Position contrast: Яна (Yana) vs мясо (myaso)
  • Words: я (I), ясно, мяч, ряд

Teacher script (quick)

  1. “Word start: ‘ya.’ After consonant: soften + ‘a’ (mya/lya).”
  2. Echo chain: мя—ня—ля (feel the soft start).
  3. Build quick words on board and clap stress.

Tip: When introducing Ю/Я (and Е/Ё), explicitly show both roles—y-vowel at word start/after vowels/Ь vs. softening marker after consonants.
