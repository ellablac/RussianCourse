Now add: С с

In English, the letters k, c, s can represent /k/ or /s/ (e.g., cat vs. center). In Russian, it’s simpler:

  • К к is always /k/ (like kit).
  • С с is always /s/ (like sun or English C in Celsius).

So Russian doesn’t need an extra letter like English C. You’ll use К for /k/ and С for /s/ every time.
