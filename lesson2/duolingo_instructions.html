1) How Duolingo is organized

Duolingo content is unlocked in a sequence. You move forward step by step.

Structure:
  • Course → Russian
  • Sections → big parts of the course
  • Units → groups of related topics
  • Lessons → short activities you complete

Tip: If you don’t see future content yet, it usually means you haven’t completed enough of the current unit. That’s normal.

2) Lessons + earning XP

A lesson is a short set of exercises (reading, listening, writing, sometimes speaking).

You earn XP by:
  • Completing new lessons
  • Reviewing lessons you already did
  • Doing practice lessons

XP per lesson can vary, but many lessons are around ~10 XP. Don’t worry about the exact number.

3) Hearts, mistakes, and practice

Hearts are Duolingo’s way to encourage careful learning. Mistakes are expected.

When you run low on hearts, Duolingo may offer:
  • Use gems to refill hearts
  • Practice to earn a heart (recommended)
  • Paid options (“extra life”, premium prompts)
For this class: Choose Practice to earn a heart.
If you couldn’t finish a lesson, you need the extra practice anyway. Practice restores hearts and helps you move forward.

4) Leaderboard + XP goals

After you complete at least one assignment/lesson, you will appear on the class leaderboard.

Leaderboard basics:
  • Shows our class and each student’s XP total
  • XP reflects activity (not perfection)
  • Reviewing old material still earns XP
Goal: I will assign XP goals, and you can earn as many XP as you want.
The key is consistent practice. For example, 10 XP a day for 7 days is better than 100 XP in one day and then nothing.

Free vs paid (what you need to know)

You can complete everything for this class for free. You do not need to enter a credit card.

Duolingo will sometimes show upgrade prompts (Super/Premium). That is normal marketing.

Free version includes:
  • All assigned lessons
  • XP and leaderboard participation
  • Practice lessons (including practice to earn hearts)
  • Progress through sections and units
Paid features (optional):
  • Unlimited hearts / fewer restrictions
  • Fewer ads and extra convenience features
  • Occasional “extra life” style prompts
If you see an upgrade screen, look for Not now, Maybe later, or an X and continue. Only pay if you personally want premium features for your own use.
